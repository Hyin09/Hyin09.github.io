About Me

Hola! I am Hang, currently a first-year Ph.D. student at the University of Guelph. I grew up in a small town, Zhangjiagang (张家港), located in the Eastern part of China. Since a young age, I have always been fascinated by the complex dynamic between nature, built environment, and us human-beings. During my second year in a local traditional high school, I got the opportunity to reroute my life completely and started my adulthood in Montréal, Canada, in an Environmental Sciences prgram at McGill University in August 2015. I dabbled in Geology for a year before finding my true passion in Atmospheric Sciences, where I completed my Bachelor and Masters degrees supervised by Prof. Andreas Zuend. After spending seven years at McGill, I moved to Guelph, Ontario in September 2022, to continue my Ph.D. journey in Environmental Sciences in the group of Prof. Scott Krayenhoff.
